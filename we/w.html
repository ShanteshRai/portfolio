Summary

  • Role: CFD Simulation Lead
  • Institution: School of Engineering & Technology, BML Munjal University, India
  • Research: Development of an advanced air filtration device, a project aimed at enhancing particle capture through impaction-based mechanisms and simulation-driven design improvements.

Key Responsibilities and Contributions

  • Developing and optimizing CFD models of the device using ANSYS Workbench.
  • Conducting CFD simulations to analyze the performance of the device under varying conditions. Validating the simulations against experimental data.
  • Collaborating with a multidisciplinary team to coordinate the development of the experimental apparatus and the simulation model in real-time.
  • Documenting and analysing the simulation data, summarizing the findings and insights from his work.

Tools Used

  • FreeCAD (CAD editing)
  • ANSYS Spaceclaim/ Mesh (pre-processing)
  • ANSYS Fluent (simulations)
  • MS Excel (data-analysis)

Learnings

  • Gained hands-on experience in multiphase and particle-laden flow simulations in Ansys Fluent, using either Lagrangian-approach (DPM) or Eulerian-approach (VoF/ Mixture/ Eulerian-Eulerian models) or both.
  • Implemented 6Dof solver with dynamic meshing to simulate wall motion dependent on fluid forces.
  • Refined my meshing skills.
  • Independently devised the simulation strategy and brain-stormed ideas to execute it with available resource.
  • Planning and sharing ideas with team members from different disciplines.
