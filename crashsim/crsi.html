Frontal Offset Crash Simulation of an SUV Ladder Chassis

Exploring ladder chassis design and crash simulations

The What and The Why

I embarked on a personal project to enhance my understanding of ladder chassis design theory and learn how to model it. Additionally, I aimed to familiarize myself with LS-DYNA for crash simulations and apply these skills to the chassis model I created. The project was divided into two parts to comprehensively explore different aspects of crash simulations. In Part I, I simulated the chassis under frontal offset collisions at 64 kmph and 100 kmph using shell elements. In Part II, I compared the performance of shell and thick-shell (tshell) elements at 100 kmph. By simulating the chassis under these conditions, I aimed to gain practical experience in automotive safety engineering and structural analysis.

The How

  • CAD and Pre-Processing: I developed a 3D CAD model of the SUV ladder chassis in FreeCAD. I prepared the mesh using LS-PrePost with shell elements and defined materials, constraints, welding, and contact definitions.
  • Simulation Setup (Part I): I created LS-DYNA input files for frontal offset crash scenarios at 64 kmph and 100 kmph, specifying the rigid wall barrier and setting a termination time of 20ms.
  • Simulation Setup (Part II): I focused on comparing shell and thick-shell (tshell) elements. A third FEM model with tshell elements was created, and simulations were conducted at 100 kmph with a termination time of 5ms.
  • Analysis: Generated stress contours, system energy plots, and rigid wall normal force plots using LS-PrePost, identifying regions of stress concentration and deformation.

The Outcome

  • Part I: I identified critical areas of deformation and stress concentration in the chassis at different impact speeds. The system's total energy was conserved, verifying the simulation setup.
  • Part II: The tshell elements exhibited higher stress concentrations and required significantly more computational time compared to the shell elements. The tshell model also had a higher maximum stress.

This project demonstrated the trade-offs between different modelling approaches and provided valuable insights into the crashworthiness of the SUV ladder chassis.

Learnings

This project was an incredible learning experience for me, especially given the constrained computational resources. I gained valuable insights into the complexities of crash simulations and the practical challenges involved in modelling high-speed collisions. Through this project, I learned about ladder chassis design and theory, modelled my own chassis using FreeCAD, and became proficient in setting up crash simulations on LS-Dyna. Completing the pre-processing using LS-PrePost was particularly rewarding. I also understood the importance of the Johnson-Cook material model for high-velocity impact analysis. Overall, this project significantly enhanced my practical knowledge in automotive safety engineering and structural analysis.
