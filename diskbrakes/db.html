Comparative Thermal Analysis of Radial and Directional Disk Brakes under High-Speed Operation

A Conjugate Heat Transfer Study

The What and The Why

The study investigates the thermal performance of two types of brake rotors: radial (straight vane) and directional (curved vane) disk brakes. The primary objective is to understand how these designs dissipate heat under high-speed conditions, which is crucial for preventing brake failure and ensuring vehicle safety. Ventilated brake discs are used due to their superior heat management capabilities, and the study aims to compare the heat dissipation efficiency of radial and directional rotor.

The How

  • Approach: Conjugate heat transfer, combining thermodynamics and fluid dynamics principles.
  • Tools: FreeCAD for modeling and Simscale for simulation.
  • Setup: Identically-dimensioned steel rotors, steady-state condition with Moving Reference Frame (MRF), simulating a car at 252 km/h (70m/s) with air entering the disks at the same speed.
  • Focus: Final temperature values and convective heat fluxes to compare thermal dissipation.

The Outcome

The results show that directional disk brakes have superior thermal efficiency compared to radial disk brakes. The maximum, average, and minimum temperatures for directional disks are lower than those for radial disks, indicating better heat dissipation. Specifically, the directional disk rotor achieves a higher air flow-rate, functioning like a turbine to enhance convective heat transfer. The study concludes that directional rotors are more effective for high-speed operations due to their better airflow and heat dissipation capabilities, making them more suitable for motorsport or racing applications.

Learnings

Through this project, I mastered thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, and CAE tools like FreeCAD and Simscale. By comparing radial and directional brake rotors through conjugate heat transfer analysis, I enhanced my skills in data analysis and problem-solving. Despite limited computational resources, this demonstration project deepened my understanding of effective heat dissipation mechanisms and equipped me to tackle real-world engineering challenges with confidence.
