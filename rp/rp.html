Determination of Aerodynamic Vibration Excitation on UAV Rotors Using CFD Simulations

The What and The Why

Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) are increasingly used in various fields like photography, agriculture, and logistics, but they face significant challenges due to vibrations from their thrust units. These vibrations can interfere with onboard sensors and compromise the structural integrity of the UAV. This research project aims to identify and analyze the main sources of vibration excitation, specifically focusing on aerodynamic turbulences around the propellers. The objective is to develop and validate computational fluid dynamics (CFD) models to study these vibrations and propose solutions to mitigate them.

The How

  • Literature Study and Model Development: Conducting a thorough review of existing methods for propeller flow simulation and vibration analysis, followed by the development and refinement of rotor flow models using ANSYS FLUENT, based on previous projects with minimal thrust and moment errors (1-5%) compared to experimental data.
  • Domain Modelling and Meshing: Creating a computational domain with distinct rotor and stator zones and generating polyhedral and hexahedral meshes with local refinements to capture crucial flow features. Mesh convergence studies were performed to ensure accuracy.
  • Simulation and Solver Setup: Setting up transient simulations using the sliding mesh technique to accurately capture unsteady aerodynamic effects, and validating the results with experimental data. Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) was applied on the thrust and moment curves to analyze vibration levels and identify critical frequencies.
  • Focus: Thrust and moment values generated the propeller.

The Outcomes

  • Model Accuracy: The Transition-SST turbulence model provided the most accurate results for low Reynolds-number flows, surpassing the performance of the k-Ï‰ SST model.
  • Mesh Convergence: The simulations showed that refining the mesh beyond a certain point resulted in negligible improvements in accuracy, indicating mesh insensitivity and convergence.
  • Vibration Analysis: FFT analysis revealed the presence of significant vibration frequencies, with the primary amplitude appearing at 200 Hz, consistent with theoretical expectations.
  • Design Insights: The identified vibration frequencies can guide the design of UAV components to avoid resonance, enhancing stability and sensor accuracy.

Learnings

Additionally, I honed my project management, problem-solving, and critical thinking abilities by tackling complex challenges, optimizing models, and utilizing remote servers and HPC for simulation work. Enhanced collaborative and communication skills were developed, since covid lockdown forced to incorporate remote workflow.
